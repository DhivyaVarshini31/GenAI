According to an NCB press release, officers of the Chennai Zonal Unit, in coordination with the Telangana Police, carried out a multi-state operation on May 12 and intercepted a container truck at the Nallur toll plaza near Chennai.
During the search, officials discovered the contraband concealed inside a specially designed false ceiling and hidden cavities built into the body of the container vehicle. The truck was seized along with the narcotic substance.
The arrested persons are natives of Odisha and Bihar. Officials said the seized ganja was being transported from Malkangiri district in Odisha and was allegedly headed for Madurai in Tamil Nadu. Preliminary investigation revealed that the traffickers had planned to move the consignment through multiple States to evade law enforcement agencies before delivering it to a receiver based in Madurai.

The NCB said the seizure had disrupted a major supply chain operating between Odisha-Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu. However, efforts are still under way to apprehend the alleged mastermind of the network and owner of the seized vehicle, who has been traced to Odisha.

Officials added that further investigation was in progress to identify other members of the syndicate, trace financial transactions linked to the operation, and dismantle the interstate narcotics network.
